### **Headline: The Great Betrayal: How Immigrants are Saving Portugal's Economy While its Government Tries to Push Them Out**


**Sub-headline:** As the government's policies torch the national budget surplus, a staggering €3 billion net contribution from immigrants is keeping the lights on. Their reward? A new law that creates instability and tells them they are not welcome.


**(Article Body)**


The story of Portugal's economy in 2025 is one of a dangerous, unfolding contradiction. It’s a story felt in every company struggling to hire and in the corridors of power in Lisbon, where a fiscal fire has been raging. But most of all, it’s a story of a profound betrayal against the very people who are keeping the country afloat.


**The Crisis is Here, Not Coming**


Let's start with the reality on the ground. This is not a future projection. A **ManpowerGroup survey for 2025** reveals a shocking statistic: **84% of employers in Portugal report difficulty finding the skilled talent they need.** This is a present-day economic crisis, crippling our ability to compete and grow.


This crisis was not a surprise. As far back as 2017, a landmark study by the Fundação Francisco Manuel dos Santos, **"Migrations and Demographic Sustainability,"** sounded a clear alarm. It warned that without significant immigration, Portugal faced a long-term deficit of up to **2.6 million jobs**, with a critical need for over a million university-educated professionals. The study's conclusion was unequivocal: immigration is "vital for the country's sustainability."


The warning was ignored.


**The Government's Fiscal Fire and the Immigrant Fire Brigade**


Instead of shoring up our future, the government's recent policies lit a fiscal fire. In a single year, populist tax cuts and spending hikes caused the national budget surplus to **plunge by a staggering 95%**, as reported by Reuters.


So why didn't Portugal fall into a deficit? Who plugged the multi-billion-euro gap?


The answer is undeniable, confirmed by the highest authorities: **Immigrant workers.**


While the government's policies were draining the treasury, immigrants were filling it. According to the latest official data from the Ministry of Labour (MTSSS), in 2024 foreign residents contributed a record **€3.6 billion** to the Social Security system. They received just **€687 million** in benefits.


This is a **net profit for the Portuguese state of nearly €3 billion in a single year.**


This isn't a fringe theory. The **Banco de Portugal** explicitly stated in its December 2024 bulletin that immigrants are directly "mitigating labour shortages." The **OECD** confirms their fiscal impact is "markedly positive." They are the engine room of our economic resilience.


**The Betrayal: A Slap in the Face**


And what is the reward for this? What is the government's "thank you" to the group that is funding our pensions, filling our jobs, and preventing a deficit?


A new law designed to create maximum instability and uncertainty. A law that doubles the residency requirement for citizenship to 10 years, sending a clear message: you are temporary, you are replaceable, you are not secure here.


As the initiators of the petition that has gathered over 12,000 signatures, we want to be clear: we are not strangers demanding special treatment. We are legal residents—engineers, doctors, entrepreneurs, and artists—who chose Portugal as our home. We live here, we work here, and our children attend Portuguese schools. We can and do prove our integration. Our petition simply asks for the right to be considered for citizenship after five years of proven legal residency and contribution, a right that was part of the stable, predictable framework that attracted us here in the first place.


This new law is an act of economic self-sabotage. It is a direct threat to the 84% of businesses that cannot find workers. It is a threat to the financial stability of our pension system. It is a betrayal of the social and economic contract that has kept Portugal afloat.


This is not a debate about immigration. This is a fight for Portugal's economic sanity. This law must be seen for what it is: **The Great Betrayal.**
