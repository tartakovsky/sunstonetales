# Initial Q&A for Liska Saves for Kebab

- **Q: What is the single problem the story will address?**
- A: Liska struggles with impulse spending, unable to save her beads for a larger reward like a fish kebab. Specifically it's the difference betweetn immediate and delayed gratification.

---

- **Q: Is any emotion central to the story, or are they used transitively for the narrative?**
- A: Emotions like frustration and disappointment are central to the story, as they drive Liska's journey to learn self-control and delayed gratification.

---
- **Q: What is the key emotion the problem triggers?**
- A: Frustration and sadness from not being able to afford her favorite kebab after spending her beads impulsively.

---

- **Q: What are the social consequences?**
- A: Liska's inability to save creates a personal challenge, but it also prompts supportive interaction with her father, who helps her learn to save.

---

- **Q: What is the one solution the story will lead to?**
- A: Liska learns to save her beads using a beautiful savings jar as a tool, which helps her resist temptation and achieve her goal of buying a kebab.

---

- **Q: What specific skill will the child learn to move toward the solution?**
- A: The skill of delayed gratification and using tools (like the savings jar) to manage impulses and save for bigger rewards.

---

- **Q: What metaphor will represent the problematic emotion or behavior?**
- A: Impulse spending is represented by the immediate lure of small treats like meat pastries, which distract Liska from her larger goal.
