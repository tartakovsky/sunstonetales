**Phase 1: The Setup - The Children's Market**

*   **Scene:** The story opens on the morning of the annual Children's Market, a special town-square fair. The rules are established: it's a place where children, and only children, can trade things they have made themselves. Each child gets a little stall to be a vendor, but they are also free to mingle and browse. The air is buzzing with excited, creative energy.

She imagines all the grand adventures he's destined for. Her goal is clear: to trade her precious Ferdinand for something equally wonderous at the market.

**Phase 2: The Initial Strategy & Twofold Failure**

*   **Scene:** Liska arrives at her stall. She is surrounded by a whirlwind of color and sound. Other children have brought brightly painted creations and items woven with shiny thread. She places her plain, natural wood fox in the center of her stall.
*   **Failure 1 (Passive Waiting):** She stands hopefully, expecting the unique charm of her fox to draw a crowd. Children walk past, their eyes drawn to the more vibrant stalls. Her hopeful smile begins to fade.
*   **Failure 2 (Hesitant Selling):** She takes Ferdinand and walks through the mingling crowd. "Would anyone like to trade for a fox?" she asks hesitantly. **Phase 4: The Search for a Home - A False Start & A Closer Look**

*   **The First Attempt (The False Start):** Liska takes a deep breath. *A home for my fox.* She scans the crowd and sees a girl with an elaborate dollhouse. "Aha! A house!" She rushes over. "Does your fox want to live in your house?" The girl looks at Ferdinand, then at her castle filled with tiny glass ponies. "No," she says. "It's a castle, not a forest. And ponies are afraid of foxes." Liska feels a fresh wave of disappointment. This is harder than she thought.
*   **The Second Attempt (The Closer Look):** Liska almost gives up, but she remembers Tahrra's words: *look for a home.* This time, she doesn't just glance at toys. She looks at what the other children are *doing*. She looks past the obvious and notices Lynx Cub, a boy she knows, alone at the edge of the market. He isn't trying to trade; he's completely absorbed in creating a miniature world on a large piece of bark.
*   **The Approach & The Revealed Need:** Because she's helped him before, she feels brave enough to approach. She doesn't mention the fox. "That looks like a whole world," she says. Lynx Cub looks up. "It's the Great North Woods," he whispers. "But it's empty. There's no one to explore it."

**Phase 5: The Empowered Proposal**

*   **The "Aha!" Moment:** For Liska, everything clicks into place. This isn't just a world; it's a *home*. Ferdinand isn't plain; he is a rugged north woods explorer. His value is defined by this context.
*   **The Active Offer:** She pulls the fox from her pocket. "My fox is a very brave explorer. He could live here." She places him in the mossy diorama, where he looks perfectly at home.
*   **The Specific Ask:** Lynx Cub's eyes go wide with delight. As he looks at the fox, Liska spots a small, beautifully smooth, crescent-shaped piece of wood lying next to the diorama. Now confident, she points to it. "For the fox," she says clearly, "I would like to trade for your wooden moon."
*   **The Joyful & Fair Exchange:** Lynx Cub nods enthusiastically. The trade feels powerful and fair to both. He gets his explorer; she gets her moon.

**Phase 6: Success Redefined as Shared Creation**

*   **The Final Scene:** Liska takes her new wooden moon, but she doesn't walk away. She props it up on the bark. "An explorer needs a moon to guide him," she suggests. Lynx Cub agrees. The story ends not with them admiring their separate new items, but with both of them huddled together over the Great North Woods, beginning a new, shared adventure for Ferdinand the Fox. The successful trade was not the end goal; it was the beginning of a friendship.
