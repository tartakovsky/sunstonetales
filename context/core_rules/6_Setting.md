
# Locations

## **The Beach**

A wide beach of golden sand meets a calm, turquoise sea. This is not an open expanse; the beach is bordered on one side by a sheer, dense wall of tropical jungle. The sounds of the ocean waves mingle with the calls of unseen jungle creatures. From the shore, the golden domes and terraced rooftops of the city are visible rising above the jungle canopy, a constant reminder of the civilization nestled against this wild edge.

**Key Details to Include:**
*   **Contrast:** The sharp line between the manicured sand and the dense, "wall" of jungle.
*   **Sensory Mix:** The smell of salt air mixed with damp earth and tropical flowers.
*   **Soundscape:** The overlapping sounds of waves and jungle fauna.
*   **View:** The city skyline visible over the trees.

## **The Main Character's Home (The "Deep")**

The interior of the home is a cool, dim sanctuary designed for communal rest. Light filters through intricate wooden latticework and panels of colored glass, casting shifting, jewel-toned patterns across the thick, clay-brick walls. The space is organized with shared living areas and sleeping spots in softly rounded alcoves, avoiding sharp corners. Children sleep not in beds, but on thick, woven blankets and cushions arranged on the floor within these curved nooks. An internal staircase often leads to a flat rooftop garden, an open-air living space used for socializing and sleeping on warm nights.

**Key Details to Include:**
*   **Lighting:** Dim, cool, and patterned by latticework and colored glass.
*   **Architecture:** Thick walls, high ceilings, and softly rounded alcoves instead of sharp corners.
*   **Furnishings:** Communal cushions and mats; children sleep on thick blankets on the floor in the nooks.
*   **Key Feature:** The transition from the enclosed, private "deep" to the open, social rooftop garden.

## **A Communal Daycare Space**

This is an informal, lively childcare space, located either in a shaded neighborhood courtyard or a large, open-walled room on the ground floor of a residential building. It is a scene of organized chaos, where a volunteer caregiver oversees a mixed-age group of young, cat-like kits. The air is filled with the sounds of storytelling, with older children teaching younger ones simple songs or observational games. Learning happens through play and peer-to-peer sharing, not formal lessons, creating an atmosphere that is energetic, chaotic, but safe.

**Key Details to Include:**
*   **Setting:** Informal and communal—a courtyard, alley, or open room, not a formal schoolhouse.
*   **Activity:** Mixed-age children learning from each other through storytelling, songs, and games.
*   **Atmosphere:** Energetic, chaotic but safe, supervised by a respected community volunteer.
*   **Population:** Young, cat-like children ("kits") of various ages interacting together.

## **City Streets and Public Spaces**

The city's streets are bustling, social corridors, shaded by canvas awnings stretched between buildings and the ornate, carved-wood balconies overhead. It is common to see individuals or small groups—students, workers, friends—stop on the steps of a public building or a wide plaza to eat. They sit, unfold a cloth bag that doubles as a placemat, and eat their meal of cured meats and bread. These public spaces are treated as an extension of the home, filled with a constant, low hum of conversation and activity.

**Key Details to Include:**
*   **Architecture:** Shaded walkways, canvas awnings, carved-wood balconies, public benches and steps.
*   **Cultural Practice:** The specific ritual of unfolding a cloth food-bag to eat a meal in a public space.
*   **Population:** People of all ages using the space for social connection and daily needs, not just transit.
*   **Atmosphere:** A constant, low hum of conversation and activity; a sense of shared ownership of public areas.

## **The Neighborhood Market**

The neighborhood market fills a sun-drenched open plaza, bordered by the golden sandstone walls of residential buildings. Instead of a maze of alleys, the space is organized around freestanding stalls built from rich tropical hardwoods. A patchwork of colorful canvas awnings is stretched between the stalls and nearby carved-wood balconies, creating shifting patterns of light and shade on the stone pavers below. The air is thick and humid, a lively mix of scents—cinnamon and cardamom from spice merchants, savory smoke from a food vendor grilling meat, and the sweet, damp smell of exotic fruits and flowers brought in from the jungle. The cat-like inhabitants gather here, their animated, expressive voices creating a constant, energetic hum as they socialize and trade.

**Key Details to Include:**
*   **Setting:** An open plaza or square, not a labyrinth. It is bordered by the characteristic golden sandstone buildings with their carved balconies.
*   **Structure:** Freestanding stalls made of tropical hardwood. The shade comes from a patchwork of colorful canvas awnings stretched overhead.
*   **Sensory Details:** The mix of smells (spices, grilled meat, jungle produce) and the energetic hum of expressive, conversational haggling.
*   **Atmosphere:** Communal and lively, more like a social hub than a purely commercial zone. It feels integrated into the daily life of the surrounding neighborhood.

## **The Backyard Garden & Sunning Spot**

Accessed through a carved wooden doorway from the main house, the backyard is a private, walled sanctuary enclosed by golden sandstone. The space is filled with lush, broad-leafed tropical plants and fragrant flowering vines that climb the warm stone walls. A small, simple pond lined with smooth river stones sits in one corner, its surface reflecting the sky.

The heart of this garden is the family's sunning spot: a specific patch of soft grass that reliably catches the warm afternoon light. A large, thick woven mat is permanently laid out here, often piled with an assortment of plush cushions. This is not just a random patch of lawn, but an intentionally cozy space designed for connection. It is where the entire cat-like family gathers to curl up together, groom, nap, and share quiet, intimate moments, away from the bustling activity of the city.

**Key Details to Include:**
*   **Enclosure & Privacy:** The garden is walled with golden sandstone, creating a safe, enclosed feeling that contrasts with the public nature of the city streets.
*   **Flora & Fauna:** A mix of lush tropical plants, flowering vines, and a small, simple stone-lined pond.
*   **The Sunning Spot's Location:** A specific, intentional area of the garden that receives direct, warm sunlight.
*   **Furnishings:** The spot is defined by a large, woven mat and comfortable cushions, marking it as a prepared, cultural space for rest.
*   **Atmosphere:** The primary feeling is of intimacy, safety, and quiet family bonding. It is a place for shared, peaceful inactivity.

## **The Communal Green**

Imagine that the private, walled backyard garden has a *second* gate at its far end—a simple, arched gate of weathered brick or sandstone, just like in the second image. This gate does not open onto a city street, but onto a wide, grassy clearing that slopes gently towards the jungle's edge.

This is the Communal Green. It is not a manicured public park, but a semi-wild meadow shared by the handful of houses whose garden walls back onto it. The grass is soft and sun-bleached, dotted with a few lone palm trees and hardy tropical shrubs. It's a place where a child can set up a small wooden table to draw, feeling the freedom of an open field while still being just steps from the safety of her own garden gate. In the distance, the dense wall of the jungle is visible, and the sound of the nearby sea is carried on the warm, orange-hued evening breeze.

**Key Details to Include:**
*   **The Transition:** The arched gate is the crucial element. It's the portal between the private, controlled garden and this larger, freer space.
*   **Landscape:** A sun-bleached, grassy clearing, not a perfect lawn. It should feel natural and a bit untamed, with lone palms and shrubs.
*   **Boundaries:** It is bordered on one side by the sandstone garden walls of several homes and on the other by the edge of the jungle. It is semi-enclosed and feels safe.
*   **Atmosphere:** A feeling of intimate freedom. It's a personal slice of the wild, safe enough for a child to play alone. The warm, golden light of sunset is particularly important here.
*   **Ownership:** It is a semi-private space, belonging to a small community of neighbors. This reinforces the communal values of the culture.
