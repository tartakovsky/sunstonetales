# Liska's Automatic Coping Behaviors

> We need to keep only two per emotion and consistently showcase only them

This document lists brainstormed automatic (instinctive) coping behaviors for Liska across various emotions as defined in `emotional_metaphors.md`. These are not body tells or learned behaviors but instinctive comfort actions (Item 3 in the emotion unfolding sequence). Each emotion includes options, with some finalized to 2 selected behaviors based on user feedback, while others retain 5 options for further discussion. All behaviors aim to balance realism and desirability for a 4-6 year old audience, ensuring developmental appropriateness.

## 1. Anger (Internal Pressure)
- **Stomping in Place**: Liska stomps her feet hard on the ground to release pressure. Realistic as a physical outlet, desirable as it avoids harm.
- **Squeezing a Nearby Object**: She grabs something soft (like a leaf or cloth) and squeezes it tightly. Realistic as a common child response, desirable as it redirects energy.
- **Turning Away and Hiding Face**: She spins around and covers her face with paws to block the trigger. Realistic as withdrawal, desirable as it prevents escalation.
- **Puffing Out Air Loudly**: Liska blows out air in short, sharp bursts to let off steam. Realistic as a natural reaction, desirable as a non-destructive release.
- **Kicking at the Ground**: She kicks at dirt or sand lightly to vent frustration. Realistic for impulsive reaction, needs scrutiny for desirability.

## 2. Joy (Warmth & Clarity)
- **Spinning in a Circle**: Liska spins around with arms out, letting happiness flow. Realistic as spontaneous expression, desirable as safe and joyful.
- **Clapping Paws Rapidly**: She claps her paws together quickly to express delight. Realistic for young children, desirable as harmless.
- **Hopping Up and Down**: She hops repeatedly in place to release joyful energy. Realistic and common, desirable as safe.

## 3. Sadness (Heaviness & Tears)
- **Curling Up Small**: Liska sits down and pulls knees to chest, making herself small. Realistic as withdrawal, desirable as non-harmful.

- **Self-Hugging with Tail Inside Hug**: She wraps her arms around herself, including her tail in the hug for comfort. Realistic as a self-soothing gesture, desirable as it shows self-care and is safe.

## 4. Hurt (External Impact)
- **Rubbing Chest Gently**: Liska rubs her chest where the 'sting' hit to soothe herself. Realistic as self-comfort, desirable as non-destructive.
- **Stepping Back Physically**: She takes a small step back to create space. Realistic as protective, desirable as avoids confrontation.
- **Holding Breath Briefly**: She holds her breath to steady herself. Realistic as a pause, needs scrutiny for subtlety or panic risk.
- **Turning Sideways**: She turns her body sideways to shield from hurt. Realistic as defensive, desirable if not full withdrawal.
- **Pointing a Paw Briefly**: She points at the source of injustice, then lowers it. Realistic as accusation, desirable if brief. Mouth open in shock "But... why..."

## 5. Indignation (Reaction to Hurt)
- **Stamping One Foot**: Liska stamps a foot once to release righteous anger. Realistic as protest, desirable if limited to one stamp.
- **Shaking Head Vigorously**: She shakes her head to reject unfairness. Realistic as denial, desirable as non-harmful.
- **Letting Out a Sharp Puff**: She puffs out a sharp breath to vent. Realistic as release, desirable as safe.

## 6. Simmering Offence (Aftermath of Indignation)
- **Sitting with Back Turned**: Liska sits facing away to nurse lingering hurt. Realistic as sulking, desirable if temporary.
- **Fiddling with Something**: She picks at grass or an object to distract herself. Realistic as displacement, desirable as harmless.
- **Muttering to Herself**: She mutters quietly to process offence. Realistic as venting, needs scrutiny for negative self-talk.

## 7. Shame (Internal Self-Blame)
- **Covering Face with Paws**: Liska puts paws over face to hide from shame. Realistic as desire to disappear, desirable if brief.
- **Hiding and Peeking Out**: She hides her face but peeks out slightly, as if checking if it's safe. Realistic as a childlike way to cope with shame, desirable if temporary to avoid full isolation.
- **Washing one paws with another**: like she's washing off dirt. 

## 8. Helplessness / Self-Doubt (Performance Failure)
- **Sitting Down Heavily**: Liska plops down as if giving up momentarily. Realistic as defeated, desirable if framed as pause.
- **Staring at the Problem**: She stares blankly at the task, frozen in doubt. Realistic as common, needs scrutiny for defeat emphasis.
- **Shuffling Feet Slowly**: She shuffles feet aimlessly to release energy. Realistic as displacement, desirable as non-harmful.

## 9. Fear (Freeze Response)
- **Clutching Her Tail to Chest with Both Paws**: Liska grabs her tail and holds it tightly to her chest to anchor herself. Realistic as seeking comfort in something familiar, desirable as a safe self-soothing action.
- **Backing Away Slowly**: Liska takes small steps backward to distance herself. Realistic as flight, desirable if controlled.
