
## Emotion Groups

A classification of primary emotional states.

---

### Name: Fear / Anxiety

-   Valence: Negative

---

-   Valence: Positive

## Emotional Triggers

Common situations that provoke specific emotional responses in characters.

---

### Category: Social Rejection
-   Triggers: Not being picked for team; Friends whispering without you; Being told "you can't play"; Birthday party not invited to; Sitting alone at lunch; Being ignored when trying to join
-   Emotion Groups: Sadness / Sorrow, Loneliness / Isolation, Hurt, Shame / Guilt
-   Usage Examples: "The other children ran to play chase, but no one asked Liska." "Mila and Sara whispered secrets by the well." "All the spots around the storyteller were taken."

---

### Category: Authority/Rule Breaking
-   Triggers: Getting in trouble with parent/teacher; Breaking household rules; Being punished by someone in power; Disappointing authority figures; Being scolded by adults; Having privileges taken away
-   Emotion Groups: Shame / Guilt, Fear / Anxiety, Anger / Frustration, Sadness / Sorrow
-   Usage Examples: "Papa's voice was stern: 'Liska, we don't throw stones.'" "The village teacher said Ren had to sit alone." "Mama took away the wooden toys after the mess."

---

### Category: Blame/Criticism
-   Triggers: Being blamed by friends; Someone criticizing your choices; Being told you did something wrong; Peers disagreeing with what you did; Social disapproval from equals; Being called out for mistakes
-   Emotion Groups: Shame / Guilt, Hurt, Sadness / Sorrow, Anger / Frustration
-   Usage Examples: "'You spilled the milk again!' Mama sighed." "Elder Finn shook his head at Liska's crooked stitches." "'Why can't you be careful like your sister?'"

---

### Category: Loss/Separation
-   Triggers: Pet gets sick/dies; Grandparent passes away; Best friend moves away; Favorite toy breaks/lost; Parent goes on business trip; Babysitter instead of parent
-   Emotion Groups: Sadness / Sorrow, Loneliness / Isolation, Fear / Anxiety, Hurt
-   Usage Examples: "Grandfather's chair sat empty by the hearth." "The little goat couldn't get up from the hay." "Papa packed his traveling cloak for many days."

---

### Category: Performance Failure
-   Triggers: Can't tie shoes; Bad grade; Losing game; Can't open jar; Need help dressing; Can't reach something; Being told "you're too little"; Messing up in front of class
-   Emotion Groups: Shame / Guilt, Anger / Frustration, Sadness / Sorrow, Helplessness / Powerlessness
-   Usage Examples: "Liska's clay pot cracked in the kiln again." "Everyone else could climb the apple tree." "The heavy bucket handle slipped from Ren's hands."

---

### Category: New/Unknown Situations
-   Triggers: First day of school; New babysitter; Doctor/dentist visit; Sleeping at friend's house; Moving to new house; Starting new activity/sport
-   Emotion Groups: Fear / Anxiety, Overwhelm / Stress, Confusion / Uncertainty
-   Usage Examples: "The big market square bustled with strangers." "A new healer with different remedies and tools." "Everything smelled different in the new cottage."

---

### Category: Unfairness
-   Triggers: Sibling gets bigger piece; Someone cuts in line; Being blamed for something you didn't do; Different bedtimes; Unequal privileges; Broken promises from adults
-   Emotion Groups: Anger / Frustration, Hurt, Sadness / Sorrow
-   Usage Examples: "Mila got the biggest honey cake without asking." "The boy pushed right past Liska at the well." "Papa said maybe later, but later never came."

---

### Category: Physical Discomfort
-   Triggers: Being hungry before dinner; Too tired for activities; Feeling sick; Scraped knee; Loud noises; Itchy clothes; Being too hot/cold
-   Emotion Groups: Anger / Frustration, Sadness / Sorrow, Overwhelm / Stress, Fear / Anxiety
-   Usage Examples: "Liska's belly growled during story time." "The wool tunic scratched her neck." "The thunder made Ren cover his ears."

---

### Category: Sibling Conflicts
-   Triggers: Sibling takes your toy; Gets more attention; Breaks your stuff; Tattles on you; Gets special treatment; Invades your space
-   Emotion Groups: Anger / Frustration, Jealousy / Envy, Hurt
-   Usage Examples: "Mila grabbed the wooden horse from Liska's hands." "Baby Sara got to stay up for the harvest feast." "Ren's carved animals scattered across the floor."

---

### Category: Achievement
-   Triggers: Learning to read; Riding bike without training wheels; Winning a race; Getting good grade; Helping someone; Completing puzzle
-   Emotion Groups: Happiness / Joy, Pride / Confidence, Excitement / Energy
-   Usage Examples: "The carved piece fit perfectly in the puzzle!" "Liska rode the pony all by herself down the path." "Ren sounded out every word in the prayer book."

---

### Category: Social Connection
-   Triggers: Making new friend; Being included in game; Having playdate; Family game night; Getting hugs; Someone sharing with you
-   Emotion Groups: Love / Affection, Happiness / Joy, Gratitude / Appreciation, Trust / Security
-   Usage Examples: "Sara saved a place right next to her for Liska." "Three children called Ren's name to join their game." "Grandmother's arms wrapped warm around her."

---

### Category: Unexpected Events
-   Triggers: Fire alarm; Thunderstorm; Surprise visitor; Change in plans; Loud crash; Unexpected gift; Finding lost toy
-   Emotion Groups: Surprise / Wonder, Fear / Anxiety, Happiness / Joy
-   Usage Examples: "CRASH! Something fell in the kitchen." "A wrapped bundle sat on Liska's bed." "Dark clouds covered the sunny meadow."

---

### Category: Independence Struggles
-   Triggers: Can't open jar; Need help with homework; Can't reach something; Being told "you're too little"; Needing help dressing; Unable to do what others can do
-   Emotion Groups: Anger / Frustration, Helplessness / Powerlessness, Sadness / Sorrow
-   Usage Examples: "The heavy cottage door wouldn't budge for Ren." "Liska stood on her toes but still couldn't reach the shelf." "The leather laces tangled no matter how she tried."

---

### Category: Peer Comparison
-   Triggers: Friend has better toy; Someone is faster/stronger; Others can do something you can't; Comparing drawings/work; Feeling less capable than peers
-   Emotion Groups: Jealousy / Envy, Sadness / Sorrow, Shame / Guilt
-   Usage Examples: "Mila's embroidery had perfect tiny stitches." "All the other children could skip stones across the pond." "Sara's new wooden doll had painted rosy cheeks."

---

### Category: Routine Disruption
-   Triggers: Different pickup person; Cancelled activity; Change in schedule; Substitute teacher; Eating different food; Unexpected changes to plans
-   Emotion Groups: Fear / Anxiety, Confusion / Uncertainty, Anger / Frustration, Sadness / Sorrow
-   Usage Examples: "Mama wasn't at the usual meeting spot after market." "The meadow was flooded from the rains." "A new helper worked at the baker's shop."

---

### Category: Scary/Threatening
-   Triggers: Monster under bed; Scary movie scene; Big dog; Getting lost in store; Stranger approaching; Dark basement
-   Emotion Groups: Fear / Anxiety, Overwhelm / Stress, Helplessness / Powerlessness
-   Usage Examples: "Shadows danced strangely on the cottage wall." "The big farm dog barked loudly behind the fence." "Mama disappeared between the market stalls."

---

### Category: Empathy Triggers
-   Triggers: Seeing someone cry; Friend getting hurt; Animal in distress; Someone being mean to others; Sad story; Witnessing unfairness to others
-   Emotion Groups: Empathy / Compassion, Sadness / Sorrow, Fear / Anxiety
-   Usage Examples: "Ren's eyes filled with tears by the duck pond." "The little bird couldn't fly with its hurt wing." "Someone pushed Sara down for no reason."

---

### Category: Anticipation/Waiting
-   Triggers: Waiting for birthday; Christmas morning; Vacation; Friend to arrive; Turn on playground; Results of something
-   Emotion Groups: Excitement / Energy, Fear / Anxiety, Hope / Optimism
-   Usage Examples: "Three more sleeps until Grandmother visits!" "The feast bread sat covered on the table." "Would the seeds really grow into carrots?"

---

### Category: Responsibility/Helping
-   Triggers: Feeding pet; Helping with chores; Taking care of younger sibling; Being trusted with special task; Cleaning room; Being asked to help when wanting to play
-   Emotion Groups: Pride / Confidence, Happiness / Joy, Overwhelm / Stress, Anger / Frustration
-   Usage Examples: "Liska carried the special feast bowls very carefully." "Ren got to feed the chickens all by himself." "Papa asked for help right during favorite game time."

---

### Category: Moral/Ethical Conflicts
-   Triggers: Accidentally hurting someone/something; Making choices that harm others; Feeling responsible for bad outcomes; Doing something against your values; Witnessing wrongdoing and feeling conflicted; Causing unintended consequences
-   Emotion Groups: Shame / Guilt, Sadness / Sorrow, Fear / Anxiety, Empathy / Compassion
-   Usage Examples: "The tower fell and knocked down Sara's castle too." "Liska's words made Ren's face crumple." "The cat ran when the door slammed by accident."
