# Plot Points for "From Purr to Growl"

1. **Opening scene**: Liska purring loudly while creating art, showing her capacity for joy and intense emotions.
2. **Problem introduction**: Anji keeps bothering Liska during playtime - touching her things, interrupting, being too loud.
3. **Escalation**: Multiple instances of Anji's boundary-crossing behavior causing Liska's frustration to build.
4. **Internal struggle**: Liska trying to handle her feelings by holding them in, which only makes them bigger.
5. **Crisis moment**: Liska working on special birthday drawing for her mother when Anji barges in, grabs her favorite color, and accidentally spills water on part of her drawing.
6. **Emotional explosion**: Liska growls and scratches across her drawing in anger, immediately regretting it.
7. **Vulnerable moment**: Liska runs to her parents, crying loudly, drawing ruined, feeling hurt, angry, and indignant.
8. **Validation**: Father hugs and comforts her. Acknowledges her feelings rather than punishing her - "Sounds like you had some really big feelings today."
9. **Body awareness lesson**: Father helps Liska identify her body's "early warning signs":
    - Her tail gets stiff and twitches (animal feature)
    - Her chest feels tight and heart beats faster (human-relatable)
    - Her face gets hot and her paws/hands feel tingly (both)
    - Her breathing gets shallow and quick (both)
    - Her shoulders scrunch up toward her ears (both)
    - She feels a "bubbling" sensation in her tummy (both)
10. **Three-part technique**: Parents teach "Spot, Stop, Shift" method:
    - **Spot**: Liska learns to say specific phrases: "My chest feels tight" or "My face is getting hot"
    - **Stop**: "Snow leopard breath" - breathing in slowly like smelling a flower, then out like gently warming paws
    - **Shift**: Techniques that work anywhere:
        - "Claw stretch" - spreading fingers wide, then squeezing into fists, then fully relaxing
        - Gentle pressure - pressing palms together in front of chest and pushing hard for 5 seconds
        - Silent "growl vibration" - humming very quietly while breathing out
        - Quick finger wiggle - rapidly wiggling fingers at sides to release tension
11. **Emotion sizing**: Liska learns to show emotion size by holding hands apart:
    - Hands close together: "Little feelings"
    - Hands medium distance: "Medium feelings"
    - Hands wide apart: "Big feelings"
    - Arms stretched as far as possible: "Too-big feelings"
    - She combines this with simple descriptions: "My angry feeling is THIS big right now" (showing with hands)
12. **Boundary language**: Mother teaches phrases like "I need space right now" or "Please ask before touching my things" to use BEFORE emotions get too big.
13. **Always-available calming techniques**: Liska learns methods requiring no special items:
    - Rubbing her temples gently in small circles
    - Using clothing: "I can rub the soft inside of my sleeve"
    - Using breathing: "I can take three deep snow leopard breaths anywhere"
    - Using proprioception: "I can press my paws together to feel steady"
    - Using visualization: "I can picture my special calm place in my mind"
14. **Practice through play**: Role-playing scenarios with stuffed animals to practice the techniques in low-stress situations.
15. **Real-world application**: When Anji bothers her again at school, Liska:
    - Notices her signals (hot face, tight chest)
    - Quietly uses her "paws together press" technique under her desk
    - Takes one deep breath while counting to 3 in her head
    - Uses a simple phrase: "I need a moment before I talk"
    - If needed, asks to get a drink of water (legitimate reason to step away)
16. **Creative expression**: Mother helps Liska create a "feelings journal" where she can draw her emotions after managing them, using colors and shapes to show how the feeling looked.
17. **Parent modeling**: Father demonstrates using the technique himself when frustrated with a broken tool, showing it's normal for everyone to need emotional tools.
18. **Sharing skills**: Liska teaches the techniques to Anji, building friendship through shared emotional understanding, helping him recognize when his excitement gets too big.

## Key Message Elements

- Feelings themselves are never wrong - they're just signals, information
- How we react to them and behave because of them can be bad for us
- Our bodies tell us when emotions are getting too big
- Everyone needs tools to express big feelings appropriately
- Sometimes the most beautiful creations come from our strongest emotions
- It's okay to need space and to ask for it
- Learning to manage emotions is a skill that takes practice

## **Potential Areas for Refinement (Addressing Your Concerns):**

1. **Pacing the Intro/Problem:**
    - You *could* add one more beat of Liska happily drawing for another half-page or page before Anji's interruption if you want to soften the immediate dive. For example, show her choosing colors, humming, thinking about her mom's reaction. This would make the disruption feel even more significant.
    - However, the current quick setup is also valid for getting straight to the point.
2. **Making the Teaching Section Feel Less "Preachy":**
    - **Show, Don't Just Tell (even more):** While you have Liska practicing, you could add more of her internal experience *during* the practice. "When Liska did the snow breath, she felt a little calmer. Her tail twitched less."
    - **Break it Up Visually/Pacing:** Ensure illustrations play a huge role here, making the techniques look engaging and easy to try. Each technique could be a mini-scene.
    - **Condense Slightly (If Needed):** Review if every single explanation is vital or if some could be shown more through action/illustration. For instance, the "show the size of your feeling" is good, but ensure it flows quickly.
    - **Liska's Agency:** Emphasize Liska *choosing* to try the techniques, rather than just passively receiving instruction. (She does, but you can lean into it).
