# Initial Q&A

- **Q: What is the single problem the story will address?**
- A: Liska struggles with managing big emotions when her boundaries are crossed by Anji.

---

- **Q: What is the key emotion the problem triggers?**
- A: Indignation

---

- **Q: What are the social consequences?**
- A: Tension and conflict between Liska and Anji due to boundary violations.

---

- **Q: What is the one solution the story will lead to?**
- A: Liska learns to recognize her emotional signals and use strategies to calm herself and set boundaries.

---

- **Q: What specific skill will the child learn to move toward the solution?**
- A: Liska learns the 'Spot, Stop, Shift' method to notice her body's signals, pause with a deep breath, and shift her reaction by using calming techniques and boundary-setting phrases.

---

- **Q: What metaphor will represent the problematic emotion or behavior?**
- A: Indignation is represented by a 'storm' or 'wildfire'

---

- **Q: What coping strategy will be introduced to deal with the emotion?**
- A: Techniques like 'Snow Leopard Breath', pressing paws together, and using boundary-setting phrases.
