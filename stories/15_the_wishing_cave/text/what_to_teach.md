### The Play-Oriented Observation: An Outline

This strategy focuses on initiating contact by validating the other child's ongoing activity.

**What to Do:**

1.  **Read the Feeling First:** Before approaching, check if the other child looks happy or calm (a "green light").
2.  **Make a Positive Observation:** State a simple, factual, and positive comment about their play (e.g., "Wow, that's a cool drawing."). Use positive words.
3.  **Use a Friendly Voice:** Deliver the comment with a warm, friendly tone.
4.  **Accept and Inquire:** If they correct you ("It's not a drawing, it's a map!"), accept their expertise and ask a question ("A map to what?").

**What Not to Do:**

1.  **Don't Interrupt a Bad Moment:** Avoid approaching if the child looks frustrated, sad, or deeply focused on a delicate task.
2.  **Don't Sound Critical:** Avoid a flat or judgmental tone that could be misinterpreted as an insult.
3.  **Don't Argue:** Never contradict their imaginative frame. Their world, their rules.
4.  **Don't Go Silent:** Be prepared to respond or ask a question after their initial reaction.
