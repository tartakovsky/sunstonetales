## Act 1: Introduction

### 1.1 & 1.2: Introduce Setting, Atmosphere, and Characters
Text:
Liska claims a perfect, sun-drenched patch of moss in Sarabad Park, declaring, "This is my official Sunning Spot. No un-invited paws!" Anji arrives in a chaotic tumble, his cape snagging on a bush and sending a shower of petals into the air. He lands, dizzy and giggling, right at the edge of Liska's spot. Nearby, Tahrra sits perfectly still, her golden eyes tracking the complex flight path of a single bee.

> **Review:** The introduction effectively establishes the setting and characters through dynamic action. It is well-executed and requires no changes.

## Act 2: Inciting Incident – The Challenge Begins

### 2.1: Lead-up
Text:
"First one to spot a blue butterfly wins!" Anji yowls, already scanning the flowers. Liska, confident in her sharp eyes, gives a determined nod. Tahrra offers a slow, quiet blink of assent, her gaze already sweeping the meadow.

- Slot: `Engagement`: Tension

> **Review:** The lead-up establishes a simple, fair game that relies on observation, setting the stage for a legitimate win.

### 2.2: The Catalyst (Misstep / Misjudgment / Accidental Event)
Text:
After a moment of searching, Tahrra quietly says, "There." She points a steady paw towards a cornflower, where a tiny blue butterfly is resting, almost invisible. She has won.

- Slot: `Engagement`: Surprise

> **Review:** The catalyst is a fair win by Tahrra, whose observant nature gives her an advantage. This removes any ambiguity of cheating and makes Liska's subsequent feelings purely internal.

### 2.3: The Core Incident (The External Problem / Challenge)
Text:
Anji cheers for Tahrra. Liska tries to smile, but her face feels stiff. A sour taste, like an unripe grape, fills her mouth. The fun of the game vanishes, replaced by a grumpy, tight feeling. This was envy.

- Slot: `Emotional Trigger`: `Peer Comparison`
- Slot: `Emotional Metaphor`: `Sour Grapes (Кислый Виноград)`
- Slot: `Emotion Group Triggered`: `Jealousy / Envy`

> **Review:** This is the correct placement for the `Sour Grapes` metaphor. It's triggered by a fair loss, making the emotion entirely Liska's to grapple with. There is no external injustice to blame.

### 2.4: Fallout
Text:
"I would have seen it first, but the sun was in my eyes," Liska mutters. The comment hangs in the air, souring the happy mood. Anji's cheers falter. Tahrra looks at Liska with her steady, knowing gaze.

- Slot: `Dysregulation`: `With Peers - Acting Out` (Verbal, devaluing friend's win with an excuse).

> **Review:** Liska's reaction is a mild "sour grapes" excuse. It doesn't stop the play but creates a palpable tension, showing how envy can subtly poison a shared experience.

### 3.1: Recovery (Optional)
> **Review:** This beat is integrated into the ongoing action of the next section, as Liska attempts to recover by continuing to play rather than stopping for a dedicated regulation cycle.

"Let's play again!" Anji suggests, trying to bring the fun back. "Who can make the biggest splash in the stream!" They all run to the water's edge. Liska and Tahrra find similar-sized stones and make almost identical splashes. Anji, however, trips and falls in completely with a gigantic SPLASH and a surprised yowl. He didn't win, he just got soaked, and his loud frustration makes everyone feel a bit awkward. The game ends in a tie, and Liska doesn't feel any better.

> **Review:** This attempt correctly results in a tie, denying Liska the clear win she craves to soothe her envy. Anji's outburst of frustration adds a layer of social complexity.

### 3.3: Attempt 2 (Developing Approach / New Complication)
Text:
"Let's build pebble towers," Liska says, deciding to take charge. "But you have to do it right." She starts directing Anji: "No, not that stone, it's too wobbly. Put the flat one there." Anji looks confused and loses interest, wandering off to chase a frog. Tahrra simply watches Liska, not building at all. Liska's attempt to control the game leaves her playing alone. Frustrated, she knocks over her own tower and stomps away.

- Slot: `Emotional Trigger`: `Performance Failure` (socially)
- Slot: `Dysregulation`: `With Peers - Acting Out` (Stomping away).

> **Review:** This beat shows Liska trying to manage her internal envy by exerting external control. The natural consequence is that her friends disengage, as being bossed around isn't fun. This leads directly to her self-isolation.

### 3.4: Low Point (Emotional & Practical Setback)
Text:
Liska sits by herself under the big oak tree, her back to the others. She quietly puts the tip of her tail in her mouth and sucks on it. A moment later, she hears a new burst of laughter. Tahrra is now tossing stones into the stream, and Anji is trying to catch them mid-air, tumbling and splashing with glee. Seeing them having so much fun without her makes the sour taste of envy flood her mouth again, mixed with a heavy wave of sadness.

- Slot: `Emotional Trigger`: `Social Rejection`
- Slot: `Dysregulation`: `With Peers - Withdrawn`
- Slot: `Auto-Coping`: `Sucking on her tail`

> **Review:** This is the true low point. The physical and emotional isolation, combined with seeing the joy she is missing, intensifies her envy and sadness. The tail-sucking is a key indicator of her regression.

### 3.5: Recovery
Text:
Suddenly, Anji comes bounding over. "WE WANT TO PLAY WITH YOU!" he yowls, then immediately spins around and runs back to Tahrra before Liska can even react. A moment later, Tahrra comes and sits quietly on the grass a few feet away, not saying anything, just offering a calm, steady presence.

- Slot: `Regulation Strategy`: Peer-supported regulation.
    - Slot: `Support`: `Signal Readiness` (from Anji) and `Calm Presence` (from Tahrra).

> **Review:** The recovery is initiated by peers in two distinct and character-appropriate ways. Anji provides an explicit, energetic invitation, while Tahrra provides quiet, non-judgmental co-regulation.

### 3.6: Re-evaluation
Text:
After a long silence, Tahrra speaks. "Your parents taught you what to do when the grape is sour," she says, her voice soft. "You spot it. You stop. You find a sweet berry instead." Liska looks at her friends, laughing by the stream. She had spotted the sour feeling, but she hadn't stopped it. She had let it take over. She needed to find a sweet berry.

Solution Building:
- Slot: `Solution Discovery Process`: `Remembering & Applying Past Solution` (prompted by Tahrra).
- Slot: `Support`: `Gentle Reminders` (from Tahrra).
- Solution discovered: Liska is reminded of a concrete, three-step coping strategy she already knows.

> **Review:** Tahrra's dialogue is now a profound, gentle reminder of a previously learned skill. This respects Liska's agency by empowering her to use her own tools, rather than giving her a new solution. The "Spot, Stop, Shift" idea is framed as finding a "sweet berry," connecting it to the core metaphor.

## Act 4: Climax & Solution – Overcoming Externally & Internally

### 4.1: Final Attempt / Approach (Applying New Understanding / Changed Behaviour)
Text:
Liska takes a shaky breath and walks back to her friends. "Can we play one more game?" she asks. "You can choose," she adds, her voice a little small. Anji, delighted, declares, "Let's have a hopping race to the big rock!"

- Slot: `Engagement`: Calm, Cognitive

> **Review:** Liska's final attempt is an act of social generosity, giving up the control she previously craved. This sets up a direct test of her new resolve.

### 4.2: Rising Tension / Suspense
Text:
They line up and start hopping. Anji, with his powerful legs, immediately takes the lead and wins easily, cheering as he touches the rock. This is the moment of truth for Liska. Her stomach tightens, and she feels the familiar sourness begin to creep into her mouth.

- Slot: `Engagement`: Tension

> **Review:** The tension is perfectly aligned with the core problem. The initial trigger—Anji winning—is repeated exactly. This creates a clear internal challenge for Liska.

### 4.3: The Turning Point / Final Push (Demonstrating Growth in Action)
Text:
*Spot the sour grape,* Liska thinks. She stops hopping. *Stop.* She takes a slow, deep breath, pretending to cool down hot soup. *Find a sweet berry.* She looks at Anji's happy, beaming face. "Good job, Anji," she says, and she is surprised to find that she means it. "You're a very fast hopper."

- Slot: `Engagement`: Relief
- Slot: `Coping Strategy`: `Cooling Breaths` & `Simple Self-Talk` leading to `Process Praise`.

> **Review:** This is the turning point. Liska actively uses the three-step strategy Tahrra reminded her of. She notices the feeling, uses a concrete calming technique, and then chooses a pro-social verbal action. This is a clear, powerful demonstration of emotional regulation in action.

### 4.4: Success (External Problem Resolved)
Text:
Saying the nice words feels... good. The sour taste in her mouth vanishes, replaced by something warm and pleasant. The envy is gone. She didn't win, but she doesn't feel bad. She just feels... part of the fun.

> **Review:** Success is defined internally. The resolution of the metaphor is tied directly to her successful use of the coping strategy and her pro-social action.

### 4.5: Immediate Reward / Relief (Accomplishment & Positive Emotion)
Text:
"You were fast too, Liska!" Anji cheers. "We're all fast!" Tahrra adds, "And now we are all at the rock. Together." The shared feeling of arrival and togetherness is the best prize of all.

> **Review:** The reward is immediate and peer-driven, focusing on the positive outcome of togetherness rather than victory.

## Act 5: Resolution & Conclusion – Integrating the Growth

### 5.1: Relaxation / Immediate Aftermath (Processing the Journey)
Text:
The three friends sit together on the big rock, catching their breath and watching the clouds. The tension is gone, replaced by an easy, comfortable silence.

> **Review:** A simple, calm scene showing the new equilibrium.

### 5.2: Reflection & Learning (Internalizing the Lesson & Growth)
Text:
"It's more fun when we all cheer," Liska says quietly, mostly to herself. Tahrra nods once, a tiny smile on her face.

- Slot: `Engagement`: Calm, Cognitive.

> **Review:** Liska articulates the core lesson herself, showing she has internalized the experience.

### 5.3: Making it Right / Integration (Acknowledging & Embodying Change)
Text:
Her graceful loss and genuine praise for Anji have already repaired the social fabric, strengthening their friendship more than winning ever could.

> **Review:** The repair is shown through action, not a formal apology.

### 5.4: New Status Quo / Harmonious Scene (Character Transformed)
Text:
Later, Tahrra points out a cloud that looks like a sleeping dragon. Instead of feeling a pang of wanting to have seen it first, Liska gasps with delight. "Wow, Tahrra! You're the best cloud-spotter!"

> **Review:** This provides a final, concrete test of her new skill. The explicit absence of the envious feeling shows the lesson is integrated.

### 5.5: Concluding Thought / Wrap-up
Text:
Liska learned that envy was a sour grape that made you feel alone, but cheering for a friend was a sweetness you could share.

> **Review:** This concluding thought is a powerful, elegant, and warm restatement of the core lesson, summarizing the metaphor and its resolution.
