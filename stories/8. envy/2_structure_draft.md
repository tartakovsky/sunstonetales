# Story Structure: "Liska Learns to Play Along"

## Act 1: Introduction

### 1.1: Introduce setting and atmosphere
Liska, Anji, Tahrra, and Slypaws meet at the park in Sunstone, where the atmosphere is bright and cheerful, full of excitement for an afternoon of games.

### 1.2: Introduce character(s)
The energetic friends are eager to play, and each suggests a different game.

## Act 2: Inciting Incident – The Challenge Begins
### 2.1: Lead-up
To be fair, the friends decide to vote on which game to play first.
- Slot: `Fun`: No information
- Slot: `Engagement`: Tension

### 2.2: The Catalyst (Misstep / Misjudgment / Accidental Event)
The group votes for hide-and-seek, and Liska's choice, tag, doesn't win.
- Slot: `Engagement`: Tension
- Slot (Optional): `Fun`: No information

### 2.3: The Core Incident (The External Problem / Challenge)
Disappointed, Liska pouts and refuses to join, declaring that hide-and-seek isn't as fun. This creates an awkward tension for the group.
- Slot (Optional): `Fun`: No information
- Slot: `Engagement`: Tension
- Slot: `Emotional Trigger`: `Social Rejection` (Her game idea was rejected by the group vote).

### 2.4: Fallout
Liska sits alone, feeling a pang of sadness and loneliness as she watches her friends laughing and having fun without her.
- Slot: `Engagement`: Tension
- Slot: `Emotion Group Triggered`: `Sadness / Sorrow` (stemming from disappointment).
- Slot: `Dysregulation`: `With Peers - Withdrawn` (Liska physically separates herself from the group and pouts, refusing to engage).

## Act 3: Attempts to Solve / Unravel – Facing the Problem & Inner Turmoil
### 3.1: Recovery (Optional)
The sounds of her friends' laughter make Liska feel more left out, but also curious. She realizes being alone is less fun than playing, even if it's not her favorite game.
- Slot: `Engagement`: Curiosity
- Slot: `Regulation Strategy`: Self-regulation, motivated by observing others' positive experience.
    - Slot: `Coping Strategy`: No information
    - Slot (Optional): `Fun`: No information
    - Slot: `Support`: No information

### 3.2: Attempt 1 (Initial Reaction / Less Considered Approach)
Liska joins hide-and-seek and discovers that finding creative hiding spots is exciting. The whole group has more fun with her there. Anji remarks, "Games are always more fun when we all play together!"
- Slot (Optional): `Support`: No information

### 3.3: Attempt 2 (Developing Approach / New Complication)
After hide-and-seek, the group plays tag. Liska runs as fast as she can in a race but loses to Anji.
- Slot: `Engagement`: Tension
- Slot (Optional): `Fun`: No information

<!-- failure should be here. Complaint is the failure. Low point is feeling bad because of it. -->

### 3.4: Low Point (Emotional & Practical Setback)
Liska complains the race was unfair and makes excuses. The fun atmosphere evaporates. Anji looks hurt and says, "I won, but now I'm sad because you are not happy for me."
- Slot: `Engagement`: Tension
- Slot: `Emotional Trigger`: `Performance Failure` (Losing the race).
- Slot: `Emotional Metaphor`: `Sour Grapes (Кислый Виноград)`. To cope with losing, Liska devalues the win, acting as if she didn't want it because it was "unfair".
- Slot: `Emotion Group Triggered`: `Anger / Frustration` and `Envy`.
- Slot: `Dysregulation`: `With Peers - Acting Out` (Liska complains and makes excuses, disrupting the group's enjoyment).

### 3.5: Recovery
Seeing her friends' sad faces and hearing Anji's words, Liska realizes she's ruining the fun for everyone. She takes a deep breath to calm the hot, prickly feeling inside her.
- Slot: `Regulation Strategy`: Peer-supported self-regulation.
    - Slot: `Coping Strategy`: `Cooling Breaths` (Taking a deep breath to calm herself).
    - Slot: `Support`: `Communication Bridging` (Peer-to-peer version, as Anji communicates their feelings directly and non-accusatorially).

## Act 4: Climax & Solution – Overcoming Externally & Internally
### 4.1: Final Attempt / Approach (Applying New Understanding / Changed Behaviour)
After her deep breath, Liska looks at Anji and, instead of complaining, offers a small smile.
- Slot (Optional): `Fun`: No information
- Slot: `Engagement`: Expectation
- Slot: `Coping Strategy`: The new behavior is a result of using `Cooling Breaths`.

### 4.2: Rising Tension / Suspense
Her friends watch, waiting to see what she will do next. The mood of the group hangs in the balance.
- Slot: `Engagement`: Tension

### 4.3: The Turning Point / Final Push (Demonstrating Growth in Action)
Liska says, "Good job! You were really fast."
- Slot: `Engagement`: Relief
- Slot (Optional): `Fun`: No information
- Slot: `Coping Strategy`: No information

### 4.4: Success (External Problem Resolved)
Anji's face lights up with a smile, and the tension in the group disappears instantly.

### 4.5: Immediate Reward / Relief (Accomplishment & Positive Emotion)
The game resumes joyfully, and Liska feels a wave of relief, realizing making her friend happy felt better than winning.

## Act 5: Resolution & Conclusion – Integrating the Growth
### 5.1: Relaxation / Immediate Aftermath (Processing the Journey)
The group continues playing tag with a light and cheerful energy, focusing on fun over winning.
- Slot (Optional): `Fun`: No information

### 5.2: Reflection & Learning (Internalizing the Lesson & Growth)
While taking a break, Tahrra says, "Sometimes you win, sometimes you don't, but we always have fun together!" Liska nods, truly understanding.
- Slot: `Engagement`: Calm, Cognitive.
- Slot: `Support`: Peer Support (`Gentle Guidance`).

### 5.3: Making it Right / Integration (Acknowledging & Embodying Change)
Liska's genuine congratulation has already repaired the social fabric. Her cheerful play shows the change is real, strengthening their friendship.
- Slot: `Engagement`: Empathy-focused.
- Slot: `Support`: No information

### 5.4: New Status Quo / Harmonious Scene (Character Transformed)
Liska is more resilient, laughing and cheering for others when she's tagged out, showing a new maturity.

### 5.5: Concluding Thought / Wrap-up
Liska learns that playing together and supporting each other is what makes every game fun, win or lose.
