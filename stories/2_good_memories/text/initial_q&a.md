# Initial Q&A

- **Q: What is the single problem the story will address?**
- A: Liska struggles with negativity bias, focusing on a single bad event and forgetting the day's joys.

---

- **Q: What is the key emotion the problem triggers**
- A: Frustration / Anger

---

- **Q: What are the social consequences**
- A: Lack of understanding between Anji and Liska

---

- **Q: What is the one solution the story will lead to?**
- A: Liska learns to recall and value positive memories to balance out negative ones.

---

- **Q: What specific skill will the child learn to move toward the solution?**
- A: Observing emotions without solving them. Intentionally looking for good memories. Are two skills okay or too much? Solution seems to be two-step. Notice -> Change.

---

- **Q: What metaphor will represent the problematic emotion or behavior?**
- A: Anger will be visualized as Little Vulcano metaphor

---

- **Q: What coping strategy will be introduced to deal with the emotion?**
- A: Stomping? Shaking? Roaring?

---
- **Q: What metaphor will represent the target emotion or behavior?**
- A: The skill of searching for good memories would be represented by Treasure Hunting metaphor
