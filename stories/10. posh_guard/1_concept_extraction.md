## Goal of the Story

**Instructions:** Define the story's core purpose from the reader's perspective. Address the following points to create a clear objective for the narrative.
- **What are we trying to teach the reader?** To teach a child a safe and effective strategy for what to do when they get lost in a crowded place.
- **What is the reader's problem we are addressing?** A child might get separated from their caregivers and feel overwhelming fear, leading to unsafe or unhelpful reactions like hiding or freezing.
- **What solution and approach are we offering?** The story models a "chain of help" strategy: find a stationary, safe-looking adult (like a vendor at a stall), who can then help find an official helper (like a guard). It also shows the importance of overcoming the initial fear-based instinct to hide.
- **How and why do we want to present it this way?** By externalizing the fear into a "Little Mouse" metaphor and the solution into a "Giant Snow Leopard on a mountain" metaphor, the story makes the emotional journey tangible and empowering. It validates the child's fear while showing a concrete, step-by-step path to agency and resolution, modeling both self-regulation and effective help-seeking.

## Synopsis

**Instructions:** Write a two-paragraph summary of the story. It should touch on all the main plot points, including the initial situation, the problem that arises, how the characters react, the proposed solution, and the final resolution where the character internalizes the new skill.

**A:** Lured away from her parents by the irresistible smell of kebabs in a bustling market, Liska suddenly finds herself lost and alone. Overwhelmed by fear, which she experiences as turning into a tiny, helpless "Little Mouse," her first instinct is to hide. Curled up under a stall, she realizes hiding won't bring her parents back. Remembering their rule—"find a helper"—she bravely asks the Kebab-Man for help.

The kind Kebab-Man co-regulates her, calming her down before calling for Guard Omar, an expert "finder." The Guard empowers Liska, turning her from a scared mouse into a brave "lookout" by placing her on his shoulders—her own personal "mountain peak." From this vantage point, she spots her parents, leading to a joyful reunion. Liska learns that while asking for help is a brave and powerful skill, sticking close to her parents is an even better plan.
