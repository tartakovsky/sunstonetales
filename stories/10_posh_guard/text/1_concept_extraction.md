## Goal of the Story

**Instructions:** Define the story's core purpose from the reader's perspective. Address the following points to create a clear objective for the narrative.
- **What are we trying to teach the reader?** To teach a child a concrete, actionable strategy for what to do when separated from caregivers in a crowded place, emphasizing the transition from fear-driven hiding to active help-seeking.
- **What is the reader's problem we are addressing?** A child might get separated from their caregivers and experience overwhelming fear that triggers an instinctive but unhelpful response (hiding/freezing), preventing them from taking effective action to reunite with their parents.
- **What solution and approach are we offering?** The story models a two-step help-seeking strategy: 1) Self-regulation through familiar comfort (hugging tail) to access memory of pre-taught rule, and 2) Initiating a "chain of help" by approaching a safe, stationary adult (vendor) who can contact an official helper (guard). The approach validates the fear response while providing concrete steps to overcome it.
- **How and why do we want to present it this way?** By using the "Little Mouse" metaphor for paralyzing fear and the "Giant Snow Leopard on a mountain" for empowered agency, the story makes abstract emotions tangible and memorable. This dual metaphor system helps children recognize their emotional state and visualize the transformation from helplessness to empowerment through brave action.

## Synopsis

**Instructions:** Write a two-paragraph summary of the story. It should touch on all the main plot points, including the initial situation, the problem that arises, how the characters react, the proposed solution, and the final resolution where the character internalizes the new skill.

**A:** At a bustling market in Sarabád, Liska becomes bored while her parents examine boring grown-up things. When an irresistible kebab smell captures her attention, she unconsciously follows it through a forest of adult legs, only to discover her parents have vanished. Overwhelmed by fear, she transforms from a brave snow leopard into a tiny, helpless "Little Mouse," instinctively hiding under the kebab stall counter. Her attempts to call for help fail as her mouse-like squeaks are swallowed by the market noise.

In her darkest moment of despair, Liska uses self-soothing (hugging her tail) which quiets her fear enough to hear the Kebab-Man's familiar laugh above. This triggers her memory of her parents' rule: "find a helper." Gathering courage, she tugs on his apron and asks for help. The Kebab-Man co-regulates her by placing her on a safe stool and calling Guard Omar, who empowers Liska by lifting her onto his shoulders—transforming her from mouse to "Giant Snow Leopard on a mountain peak." From this vantage point, she spots and reunites with her parents. The story concludes with Liska internalizing both lessons: asking for help is brave and powerful, but holding her parents' hands is an even better plan.
