Near the large tree on the far side of the yard, a special guest, the Glow Beetle Man, is beginning his performance.

### Beat 2.2: The Catalyst
"Liska! Over here!" Her friend Anji waved from the big blanket spread beneath the performance tree. All the neighborhood cubs had claimed the best spots, their tails swishing with anticipation. The Glow Beetle Man lifted his carved whistle to his lips, and the first low, haunting note made everyone fall silent.

From his basket emerged a living constellation. Deep violet and emerald beetles swirled upward, their lights pulsing in rhythm with the whistle's melody. The Glow Beetle Man waved his hands gracefully, directing the beetles in beautiful waves and spirals above the enchanted cubs. Then, with a gentle trill, the man coaxed forward a single beetle that glowed like a tiny sun - brilliant orange and twice as bright as the others. It floated down to perch on a kitten's (lynx cub from toothy adventure) outstretched paw, bathing his face in warm light. The kitten's eyes went wide with wonder.

Liska's heart beat so fast with wanting to touch the beetle that her paws moved on their own. She took one step, then another, her eyes locked on the tiny, glowing sun.

The yard was no longer just a path to the beetle; it was again a place of strange shadows and unfamiliar faces. A wave of fear of the unknown hits her. Her desire to touch the beetle warred with her unease. She jumped back to Mama and hid behind her leg.

## Act 4: Climax & Solution - Overcoming Externally & Internally

### Beat 4.1: Skilled Attempt

Liska slid down from Mara's lap. This time, she didn't dart or rush. She stood tall like the fox in the story must have stood. "Curiosity bigger than fear," she whispered to herself. She took one step. The ground felt solid beneath her paw. Another step. The festival sounds weren't scary anymore - they were interesting. Step by step, she began her journey across the yard.

### Beat 4.2: Minor Setback

Halfway there, the same tall stranger appeared again, still adjusting his mask. But this time, Liska saw him differently. He wasn't a giant - he was just a person at a party. Still, her heart hammered. The safe circle of Mama felt so far away. The blanket with her friends seemed even farther. For a moment, she stood frozen, caught between safety and desire.

### Beat 4.3: Persistence

The Glow Beetle Man's whistle sang out a particularly beautiful note, and the orange beetle lifted from the kitten's paw, hovering in the air like a tiny star. It pulsed brighter, as if calling to her. Liska remembered the fox creeping toward his butterfly. She looked at the stranger's legs - they were just legs, like trees in a forest. And foxes knew how to navigate forests. Instead of going back, she did something brave and clever. She said in her clearest voice: "Excuse me, please." The stranger looked down, smiled, and stepped aside with a little bow. The path opened before her.

### Beat 4.4: Success

Liska padded the final steps to the blanket, where Anji immediately scooted over to make room. "You made it!" her friend whispered. Liska settled onto the soft fabric, her heart still quick but proud. She had crossed the strange-familiar yard all by herself. The Glow Beetle Man caught her eye, smiled and winked, as if he'd been watching her journey.

### Beat 4.5: Reward -> Relief

He played a special trill on his whistle, and the brilliant orange beetle circled once, twice, then floated down toward Liska. She cupped her paws, and the beetle landed gently, its warmth spreading through her paw pads. Its glow painted her white fur gold and orange, and she felt like she was holding a piece of captured sunlight. Around her, the other cubs made soft sounds of appreciation, but Liska barely heard them. She was like a brave fox who had earned her light.
