This document maps the story "Liska's Brave Steps" to the five-act structure.

## Act 1: Introduction

### Beat 1.1: Introduce Setting and Atmosphere
The Saar's central yard, a place Liska knows intimately from daily play, is transformed for the annual "Festival of First Bloom." It's twilight. Garlands of purple and orange lamps hang between the houses, casting a magical, festive glow that mixes with the dappled light from the yard's large trees. The air is a blend of smells: spices from the Market Gate, damp earth from the Jungle Gate, and salt from the Sea Gate. Near the large tree on the far side of the yard, a special guest for the festival, the Glow Beetle Man, is beginning his performance. A large, round blanket is spread on the grass where all the other cubs and kittens are gathering. While most attendees are neighbors, a few unfamiliar faces are scattered amongst them.

## Act 2: The Challenge Begins

### Beat 2.1: Lead-up
Her friends, already on the blanket, see her and wave, calling her to join them.

The Glow Beetle Man begins his show. Using a low whistle, he coaxes a living nebula of deep violet and emerald beetles from his basket. This swirling cloud of light dances and spirals over the heads of the children on the blanket, painting their upturned faces in fleeting, magical shades. Then, with a final, soft note, the man guides a single, brilliant orange beetle—the brightest of the swarm—to separate from the cloud. It lands gently on another child's outstretched hand, pulsing with warm, soft light.

### Beat 2.2: The Catalyst
Liska is utterly captivated. She sees the look of pure wonder on the child's face, illuminated by the beetle's personal glow. In that instant, a powerful desire crystallizes: she wants *that* feeling. She wants to be close to the magic. She unconsciously takes a couple of steps forward.

### Beat 2.3: The Core Incident
A wave of separation anxiety overwhelms her powerful curiosity. The need for her safe anchor *in the presence of these unknowns* is stronger than the pull of the glowing beetle.

### Beat 2.4: Fallout
She clings to mother.

### Beat 3.6: Re-evaluation
Mara tells Liska: "I want to tell you a story. Before you were born, we cared for a tiny, snow-white fox cub. He loved being with us; our laps were his favorite place. But he was also so curious about the big, rustling forest outside, full of new sounds and strange creatures he'd never met. He wanted to explore, but he also felt anxious leaving us. He learned that his curious feeling could be just a little bigger than his anxious feeling. He learned that **his bravery was what turned the unknown into the known.** When you were born, you were a tiny ball of white fur, just like him. So we named you Liska, which means 'little fox.' We knew one day you would be a brave little explorer, just like him."

The story clicks for Liska. It reframes her anxiety. It's not a stop signal; it's a normal part of exploring. It gives her a mission: to be brave *like the fox*. She processes this, looking from Mara to the distant, magical lights of the show. She lets go of Mara and says quietly, "The fox was brave. I am Liska. I can be like the fox."

## Act 4: Climax & Solution – Overcoming Externally & Internally

### Beat 4.1: Final Attempt
She takes one deliberate step, then another. It's a "solo" journey.

### Beat 4.2: Rising Tension
She walks across the Yard, her focus locked on the swirling lights. Her anxiety transforms into determined hope. As she walks, one of the "unfamiliar" guests (perhaps an old woman) gives her a gentle, brief smile. The "unknown" becomes "known and friendly." The anxiety lessens with each step, replaced by a growing feeling of pride.

### Beat 4.3: The Turning Point

### Beat 4.4: Success
She reaches her friends on the blanket, who welcome her into the group. She finds a spot among her friends. She is immediately immersed in the wonder of the glow beetle show. The beetle man, having seen her brave journey, smiles and winks. 


### Beat 4.5: Immediate Reward/Relief
When it's time, he signals her to open her paw and guides the brilliant orange beetle to land on her outstretched palm. Liska's face is lit with that look of awe she so desperately wanted. Her joy is doubled: it's the joy of the show, amplified by the pride of her own courage. The yard is hers again, but now her ownership of it feels deeper, earned by her bravery.

## Act 5: Resolution & Conclusion – Integrating the Growth

### Beat 5.1: Catharsis/Immediate Aftermath
After the show, she runs back to Mara, her face glowing.

She doesn't just talk about the show. She says, "I was scared, but then I was curious. I took small steps... like the little fox." Mara hugs her. "I'm so proud of my brave little Liska." The new skill and identity are affirmed.
