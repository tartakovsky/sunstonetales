Do unto others as you would have them do unto you

Treat others as you would like to be treated


## Characters

- **Liska Koticheck**: Young snow leopard, struggles with emotional regulation, tends to scream when frustrated
- **Tahrra**: Introverted black panther, observant, sensitive to loud noises
- **Lucia**: Human girl, brave but doesn't speak the local language well
- **Mara Koticheck**: Liska's mother, wise and patient

## Plot Structure

### Beginning: The Beach Treasure Hunt

1. The three friends are excited about a beach treasure hunt they've planned
2. Setting: Beautiful coastal area with tide pools, shells, and hidden caves
3. Each girl brings something special:
    - Liska brings a hand-drawn treasure map
    - Tahrra brings her collection box for organizing finds
    - Lucia brings a small shovel and enthusiasm

### Rising Action: The Wonderful Discovery

1. The girls find a beautiful tidepool with colorful sea creatures
2. Tahrra carefully arranges shells in patterns
3. Lucia excitedly splashes and explores
4. Liska spots something sparkling in a small cave that's only accessible during low tide

### Problem Trigger: The Tide Turns

1. The girls realize the tide is coming in faster than expected
2. They need to work together to retrieve the sparkly object before the cave floods
3. They try to form a plan, but miscommunication occurs:
    - Tahrra wants to carefully analyze the situation
    - Lucia doesn't understand all the words but wants to rush in
    - Liska gets increasingly frustrated as time runs out

### Conflict: Liska's Outburst

1. Liska becomes overwhelmed and screams at her friends:
    - "You're both so SLOW! Just HELP ME already!"
    - "I NEED you to listen to me RIGHT NOW!"
    - "Why won't you DO what I say?!"
2. Tahrra covers her ears and backs away, sitting alone with her rock
3. Lucia looks confused and hurt, stepping back from Liska
4. The girls separate - treasure hunt ruined

### First Resolution Attempt: Mother's Wisdom

1. Liska runs home upset, telling her mother the others won't help her
2. Mara listens patiently, then asks: "How do you feel when someone screams at you?"
3. Liska admits she feels scared and wants to run away
4. Mara explains the Golden Rule: "Treat others the way you want to be treated"
5. Mara helps Liska understand her friends' perspectives:
    - Tahrra is sensitive to loud noises and gets overwhelmed
    - Lucia might not understand all the words but can feel the anger
6. Liska realizes she pushed away the very help she wanted

### First Attempt at Change

1. Next day, Liska invites friends to continue the treasure hunt
2. When the plan doesn't work, Liska feels frustration building
3. She tries to speak calmly but her voice gets louder and her tail twitches
4. The girls respond slightly better but still keep their distance

### Second Attempt at Change

1. At school, Liska wants to share her art supplies
2. When Lucia accidentally breaks a crayon, Liska feels anger rising
3. She takes a deep breath and speaks more calmly, but her body language is still tense
4. The girls accept her supplies but don't fully engage in the project

### Return to Mother

1. Liska tells her mother she tried being nicer but it didn't work completely
2. Mara explains that trust takes time to rebuild
3. "Remember how you feel after you've been upset? You can't calm down right away."
4. Mara suggests Liska not only control her voice but also:
    - Tell friends how she feels using "I" statements
    - Show with her whole body that she's calm (relaxed tail, open posture)
    - Ask what they need instead of demanding

### Final Successful Attempt

1. The girls try one last time to reach the cave before high tide
2. When things don't go as planned, Liska feels the familiar frustration
3. This time she:
    - Takes three deep breaths
    - Keeps her tail relaxed
    - Says "I feel worried about missing our chance. I'd really like your help."
    - Asks "What do you think we should do?"
4. Tahrra suggests a different approach to reach the cave
5. Lucia demonstrates a way to form a chain to reach farther
6. Together they successfully retrieve the sparkly object

### Resolution: The Golden Shell

1. The sparkly object turns out to be a rare golden shell
2. The girls decide to share it, taking turns keeping it each week
3. They create a special display box together
4. Liska realizes the real treasure was learning how to keep her friends close
5. The story ends with the three friends planning their next adventure, with Liska calmly suggesting ideas and listening to her friends' input

## Key Moral Lessons

- Treat others the way you want to be treated
- How you ask for something is as important as what you ask for
- Trust takes time to build but can be damaged quickly
- Understanding others' feelings helps you communicate better
- Friendship requires patience and respect

## Repetitive Elements (for child participation)

- Liska's "spot, stop, shift" calming technique
- The phrase "Golden Rule means golden friendships"
- Counting to three before speaking when upset

- Tahrra hides in shadows when scared
