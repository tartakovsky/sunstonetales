# Initial Q&A

- **Q: What is the single problem the story will address?**
- A: Liska struggles with understanding and coping with her parents' argument, feeling confused and sad about the tension between them.

---

- **Q: Is any emotion central to the story, or are they used transitively for the narrative?**
- A: Confusion and sadness are central to the story, as Liska experiences these emotions intensely while witnessing her parents' disagreement.

---

- **Q: What is the key emotion the problem triggers?**
- A: Primarily Confusion, followed by Fear and then Sadness as the situation unfolds.

---

- **Q: What are the social consequences?**
- A: Liska feels isolated and worried that her parents' tension might be related to her, causing a temporary rift in family connection.

---

- **Q: What is the one solution the story will lead to?**
- A: Liska learns to express her feelings and remind her family of their love and unity, helping her parents reconnect and reassuring her of their bond.

---

- **Q: What specific skill will the child learn to move toward the solution?**
- A: Expressing emotions verbally and seeking connection by reminding loved ones of shared love and family unity.

---

- **Q: What metaphor will represent the problematic emotion or behavior?**
- A: Confusion as Tangled Yarn (Запутанный Клубок).

---

- **Q: What metaphor will represent the target emotion or behavior?**
- A: There will be no metaphor for the target behavior; the focus is on the skill of emotional expression and connection.

---

- **Q: Who are the main characters in the story?**
- A: Liska, Mother (Mara), Father (Daric)
